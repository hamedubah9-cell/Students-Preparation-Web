Section 4: Properties of Nouns

Short answer + MC

‚ñ∏
11. List and briefly explain the four properties of nouns.
Answer:
‚Ä¢ Number: singular vs plural (cat/cats).
‚Ä¢ Countability: count nouns vs noncount (apple vs water).
‚Ä¢ Case/Role: grammatical role (subject/object/possessive). In English this shows in word order and possessive forms (the dog's bone).
‚Ä¢ Gender (where applicable): grammatical or natural gender (English mostly uses natural gender).
12. What is the difference between grammatical gender and natural gender in English?
Answer: Grammatical gender is a language feature that assigns nouns to classes (masculine/feminine/neuter) often arbitrarily (common in languages like Spanish). Natural gender refers to biological/social gender tied to meaning (e.g., "mother" = female). English largely uses natural gender (he/she) rather than grammatical gender for most nouns.
13. In English, the noun ‚Äúteacher‚Äù is:
Answer: D) Gender-neutral ‚Äî "teacher" can refer to any gender unless otherwise specified.
